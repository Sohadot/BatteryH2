The question is not battery or hydrogen. It is when.

Batteries dominate the short, frequent, well-aligned load. Hydrogen earns its place across the long, the infrequent, the seasonal. The value is not in either medium. It is in knowing where one hands off to the other.

The Frame

A storage decision is a classification, not a preference.

Every storage choice answers a set of governed questions. BatteryH2 names them, scores them, and produces a defensible verdict — so the decision survives an audit, a board, and a financier. Six dimensions define the crossover.

D1

Duration

How long must stored energy hold before it is needed — hours, days, or a season?

D2

Frequency

How often does the cycle repeat? Daily cycling and once-a-year reserve reward opposite media.

D3

Round-trip efficiency

Batteries return ~90%; hydrogen ~35–50%. Efficiency is decisive only when energy is scarce.

D4

Capital structure

Power cost versus energy cost. Hydrogen's cost-per-stored-hour falls as duration grows.

D5

Siting & volume

Salt caverns and geology unlock hydrogen at scale; batteries scale decentrally.

D6

Load alignment

How well does generation match demand in time? Misalignment shifts the threshold toward hydrogen.
